## Phase 3: Hypothesis Generation

### H1: Prospect Theory Framing Failure
**Observation:** "prospect theory" = 0 mentions. **Loss aversion** = 96% mentions.  
**Hypothesis:** The system prompt references prospect theory as a theoretical framework, but the LLM cannot translate it into actionable heuristics. It defaults to the simpler "loss aversion" concept.  
**Test:** Remove "prospect theory" from the system prompt and replace with explicit loss aversion parameterization (e.g., "λ = 2.25"). Measure if reasoning quality changes.

### H2: Excessive Defensiveness (Cash Drag)
**Observation:** 94.8% cash, 1 position, portfolio return -2.17%.  
**Hypothesis:** The LLM is over-optimizing for capital preservation and tail risk avoidance, leading to cash drag that erodes purchasing power. With inflation and opportunity cost, 95% cash is likely suboptimal even in defensive regimes.  
**Test:** Introduce a **minimum deployment rule** (e.g., "maintain at least 20% of capital in positions at all times, excluding crisis signals"). Backtest against April history.

### H3: Sell-Side Calibration
**Observation:** Sell accuracy (33.3%) < Buy accuracy (60.0%).  
**Hypothesis:** The loss aversion framing causes the LLM to take profits too quickly (crystallizing small gains) and cut losses too late (or vice versa). The 5-day forward window may also be too short for mean-reversion trades.  
**Test:** Extend the sell outcome window to 10 days. Compare sell accuracy. Also test adding explicit "let winners run" instruction to the system prompt.

### H4: Mean Reversion vs Momentum Regime Confusion
**Observation:** "overbought" (71%) and "oversold" (63%) are frequently cited. "mean reversion" (48%) and "momentum" (69%) both appear.  
**Hypothesis:** The LLM is sometimes contradicting itself — calling a market "overbought" (momentum signal) while expecting "mean reversion" (contrarian signal). This cognitive dissonance may lead to confused position sizing.  
**Test:** Add a regime classifier to the system prompt: "Current regime: [momentum | mean-reversion | transition]. Trade in the direction of the regime."
